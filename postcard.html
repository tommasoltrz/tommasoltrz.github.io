Interactive installation: Milano/Shenzhen Design Week 2017
POSTCARD

In a nutshell

Postcard from Casa Jasmina – Chatting with the Home of the Future is an interactive installation representing a compact version of the home of the future. The exhibition is an event born in collaboration between Casa Jasmina and ThingsCon Milan.

The installation is made of six devices, framed in simple and archetypal wooden structures, for six of the most common functions in a smart domestic setting: a temperature control system, a sensor tower, a lightning system, a speaker, a privacy-related device and a smart mirror.

Not only a collection of the best of Casa Jasmina, but first and foremost an interactive experience. Postcard from Casa Jasmina replicates a 1:1 model of a home automation system, in which all the devices can be controlled by the visitors through their smartphone.

Development

What makes it possible is a chatbot: a software that is able to simulate a conversation with the user, responding to his requests. The exhibition is based on a conversational UI system designed to make the interaction process and the chatbot’s behaviour more and more sophisticated – to the point where the chatbot itself will try to anticipate the users’ desires and needs.

The interaction between the public and the installation happens through several chat application, such as Facebook Messanger, Telegram and Wechat. The Bot, developed using Ubot and Wit.ai, would execute a sentiment analysis of the received messages and react accordingly with different house settings. The six devices are connected to each other through an open source hub called Home Assistant.

The whole project was a 4-weeks effort in which I coordinated and worked with a team composed by Edoardo Tenani (developer), Pierluigi Vona (maker) and Alessandro Squattrito (designer).
